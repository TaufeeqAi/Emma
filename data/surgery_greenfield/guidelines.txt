GREENFIELD MEDICAL CENTRE — Patient Guidelines

OPENING HOURS
Monday to Friday: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM
Saturday: 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM
Sunday: Closed
Bank Holidays: Closed

APPOINTMENT BOOKING
Same-day appointments: Available from 8:00 AM — call early
Routine appointments: Can be booked up to 4 weeks in advance
Telephone consultations: Available, request when booking
Online booking: Available via Patient Access portal

EMERGENCY PROTOCOL
If you are experiencing chest pain, difficulty breathing,
signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech problems),
or any life-threatening emergency — call 999 immediately.
Do not wait for a GP appointment.

For urgent but non-emergency concerns outside hours: call 111.

PRESCRIPTION REQUESTS
Allow 48 hours for all prescription requests.
Repeat prescriptions: Submit via Patient Access, NHS App, or in person.
Controlled drugs: Must be requested in person with ID.

REPEAT PRESCRIPTIONS
Electronic Prescription Service (EPS) available.
Nominated pharmacy: Prescriptions sent directly to your chosen pharmacy.

REFERRALS
GP referrals to hospital specialists: Processed within 5 working days.
Urgent referrals: Same day when clinically necessary.

PATIENT REGISTRATION
New patients welcome. Bring proof of address and ID.
Catchment area: Please check our website or call reception to confirm.

TEST RESULTS
Blood test and investigation results: Available 3–5 working days after testing.
Contact reception to request results — do not call before this window.

CHILDREN AND YOUNG PEOPLE
We offer specialist child health clinics every Wednesday morning.
Immunisations: Available — book through reception or NHS App.

MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT
If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, call 999 or go to A&E.
For non-emergency mental health support: contact your GP or call 111.
Samaritans: 116 123 (free, 24/7).

DISABLED ACCESS
The surgery is fully accessible: step-free entrance, hearing loop, accessible WCs.
Please inform reception of any additional needs when booking.

COMPLAINTS
We take all complaints seriously. Please speak to our Practice Manager.
NHS Complaints Advocacy is available if you need independent support.
